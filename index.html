Our Story

From a supply closet to a community anchor

In the 1980s, the Bucks County Opportunity Council kept a small food supply for clients in need. Twelve local houses of worship — the Quakertown Ministerium — stepped in, and by 2004 the effort had a name: the Quakertown Food Pantry.

The pantry became an independent 501(c)(3) in 2008, moved to the Quakertown Borough complex in 2010, and hasn't slowed down. We pick up from two Giant stores, Walmart, and Target, and we're proud members of Philabundance and BCOC.

Every person here — from the board president to the Tuesday evening shelf-stocker — is a volunteer.
